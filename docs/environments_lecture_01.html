2. Breaking up images into different parts.

  • Binary Images are used widely in Computer Vision to select areas of interest.
  • One way of producing a Binary Image is to Threshold a Greyscale image (or a R,G,B channel)
  • Image segmentation is the technical term for breaking up an image into different areas of meaning.
  • Average Brightness example. You could use this to detect the brightness of a room.
  • Thresholding example.
  • Edge detection example.
  • What else could you use these examples for?

3. Finding differences between frames of video.

  • Moving from analysing single images to the differences between frames allows a variety of new features to be detected.
  • Background Subtraction.
  • Frame Difference.
  • Motion History.
  • What else could you use these examples for?
